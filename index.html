Overview

Constructing 3D models from laser scans of Cultural Heritage (CH) sites is a vital but challenging area of research for CH preservation. These scans produce point clouds which can be visualised as 3D models.

Vital:

  • Required to record and visualise the three-dimensional structure of historical sites.
  • Can be used by preservationists to decipher where the CH site is deteriorating and needs improvement.
  • Are a means of transmitting a testimony of past human activity to future generations.

Challenging:

  • Laser scans of CH sites contain unwanted information, such as foliage, people and scaffolding, that do not belong to the CH site and which one must remove.
  • Removing this noisy data is called point cloud cleaning - a predominantly manual, laborious and time-consuming task which must be automated and made more efficient.

Clustering

Clustering partitions a dataset into homogenous subgroups called clusters, such that each comprises similar points.

Goals:

  • Maximise inter-cluster similarity
  • Minimise intra-cluster similarity
Each sub-project explores three algorithms from a different class of clustering algorithms, and uses the k-means clustering algorithm as a baseline for comparison.

  • Partitional
  • Density-Based
  • Hierarchical-Based

Results

Clustering algorithms can:

  • Augment the process of point cloud cleaning by automating it and improving its efficiency. The algorithms can, therefore, reduce the need for manual and labour-intensive cleaning.
  • Classify CH point clouds into keep and discard clusters. However, they may not be a viable solution to point cloud cleaning because they produce numerous partitions rather than one for each classification label.
Other conclusions:
  • Feature set dimensionality and selection affects clustering algorithms' performance.
  • The cluster validity metrics are insufficient for representing the usefulness of the produced clusters. Thus, selecting the optimal algorithm is complex.
  • Gaussian Mixture Model and Mean-Shift that, when joined for classification, resemble the actual ground truth labels.
